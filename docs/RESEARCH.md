## Module 1: Identity

### Core Framework
- **Values Clarification:** Based on Schwartz's Theory of Basic Human Values (1992, 2012 revision)
- **Purpose Discovery:** Ikigai framework (Japanese longevity concept)
- **Identity Development:** Erikson's Psychosocial Development stages

### Key Metrics
| Assessment | Source | Scale |
|------------|--------|-------|
| Values Survey | Schwartz Portrait Values Questionnaire (PVQ) | 40 items |
| Purpose Scale | Steger's Meaning in Life Questionnaire | 10 items |
| Identity Status | Erikson Identity Status Interview | 5 dimensions |

### Data Points
- Values: 10 basic values (Self-transcendence, Conservation, etc.)
- Purpose: Presence + Search for meaning
- Legacy: Impact statements, contribution tracking

### References
- Schwartz, S. H. (2012). An Overview of the Schwartz Theory of Basic Values.
- Steger, M. F., et al. (2006). The Meaning in Life Questionnaire.

| Sense | Exercises | Duration | Frequency |
|-------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Visual | Peripheral vision, Nature observation | 5-15 min | Daily |

| Dimension | Questions | Scale |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Exhaustion | 5 items | 0-6 frequency |
| Cynicism | 4 items | 0-6 frequency |
| Efficacy | 6 items | 0-6 frequency |

| Level | Burnout Score | Recommendation |
|-------|---------------|----------------|
| Low | 0-1.9 | Maintain practices |

## Data Quality Notes

### Self-Report Bias Mitigation
- Randomize question order
- Use validated scales
- Calibrate with behavioral data where possible
- Provide feedback on consistency

### Missing Data Handling
- Allow partial entries
- Impute missing with personal averages
- Flag inconsistent entries for review
